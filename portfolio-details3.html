Discussion

The largest obstacle to efficient human-machine teaming today is artificial intelligence (AI), which represents and models human partners. With access to nearly endless processing power from machines, humanity has the opportunity to advance to new heights. Convincing people that machines can carry out their intended tasks is the major obstacle to integrating AI in society. In the third video, I draw the conclusion that people might consider information that is off-topic in light of their own or professional experiences. AI can duplicate some of our decisions, but not all of them. What is true is that AI can assist people in becoming more human by assisting them in making more human decisions about their life. Every day, AI is already influencing our habits. To enable us to detach from our electronic temptations and have more time and space to connect with what we value most, AI cannot engage the same psychological levers that get us addicted to games or social media. After all, people have been improved and augmented ever since the first crude tools were made at the beginning of time. The only difference today is that we are pushing the boundaries of what scientists previously believed to be feasible in all areas of what it means to be a human. Humans can infer unobservable states, like situational beliefs and aims, and utilize those inferences to anticipate the forthcoming behaviors, responses, or demands of other people.
